Low Phosphorus

Diet Guide

GOAL: Limit phosphorus intake to reduce strain on the kidneys and maintain bone and heart health.
FOODS TO EAT

1. Fruits

BEST CHOICES:

Apples, Berries, Grapes, Peaches, Pineapple, Watermelon, Plums, Cherries, and Tangerines.

WHY: Naturally low in phosphorus and rich in antioxidants.

2. Vegetables

BEST CHOICES:

Cabbage, Cauliflower, Cucumbers, Bell Peppers, Lettuce, Carrots, Green Beans, Zucchini, and Onions.

WHY: Provide vitamins and fiber without adding excess phosphorus.
TIP: Boil high-phosphorus vegetables (like potatoes or spinach) to reduce phosphorus content.

3. Grains & Starches

BEST CHOICES:

White Rice, Refined Pasta, Couscous, Cream of Wheat, and White Bread.

WHY: Whole grains are nutritious but tend to contain more phosphorus. Choose refined versions instead.

4. Protein (Low-Phosphorus)

BEST CHOICES:

Egg Whites, Chicken or Turkey (Skinless, Small Portions), Lean Beef or Fish (Limited Amounts), Unsalted Canned Tuna.

PLANT-BASED OPTIONS (Moderate Portions):

Tofu, Chickpeas, Lentils (Small portions).

WHY: Provides necessary protein while minimizing phosphorus load.
FOODS TO AVOID

1. High-Phosphorus Dairy Products

AVOID:

Milk, Cheese, Yogurt, Ice Cream, and Cream Soups.

WHY: Contain large amounts of phosphorus and calcium that can build up in the blood.

2. Nuts, Seeds & Legumes

AVOID:

Almonds, Peanuts, Cashews, Sunflower Seeds, Lentils, Black Beans, and Chickpeas (In large portions).

WHY: High in organic phosphorus, which is difficult for kidneys to process.

3. Whole Grains & Bran Cereals

AVOID:

Bran Flakes, Oatmeal, Granola, Whole-grain bread, Brown Rice, and Whole Wheat Pasta.

WHY: Contain higher phosphorus than refined grains.

4. Processed & Fast Foods

AVOID:

Sausages, Hot Dogs, Deli Meats, Frozen Dinners, Instant Noodles, and Snack Bars.

WHY: Often contain phosphate additives (used as preservatives and flavor enhancers).

5. Dark Sodas & Drinks

AVOID:

Cola, Root Beer, Energy Drinks, and Some Flavored Waters.

WHY: Contain phosphorus additives in the form of phosphoric acid.
